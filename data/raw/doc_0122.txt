Dynamic programming solves complex problems by breaking them into simpler subproblems. It stores results of subproblems to avoid redundant computation. Classic examples include the Fibonacci sequence, longest common subsequence, and knapsack problem. Memoization and tabulation are two common implementation approaches. Advanced concepts in algorithms are essential for modern software development. Understanding these concepts is essential for building robust and scalable systems.
